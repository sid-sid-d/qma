Public
Liability

QMA holds public liability insurance that covers all financial members at sanctioned events and affiliated activities.

What's covered

QMA's public liability insurance policy is arranged through Australian Masters Athletics (AMA) and covers all financial QMA members while participating in sanctioned QMA events and affiliated training sessions. The policy provides third-party liability cover in the event of injury or property damage caused to another person during an insured activity.

Important: what public liability is not

Public liability insurance does not cover personal injury to the insured athlete. It covers liability to third parties. If you want personal accident or injury cover for yourself while competing, you will need separate personal accident insurance. QMA strongly encourages members to review their own insurance position, including private health insurance, before competing.

Who is covered

Cover applies to all financial members of QMA, which means paid-up members for the current membership year. Guest or day-entry participants at QMA events who are not financial members may not be covered under the QMA policy. If you are unsure of your membership status, confirm with the secretary before competing.

When cover applies

  • Sanctioned QMA competition events (all meets on the official calendar)
  • QMA-affiliated training sessions at venues approved by QMA
  • QMA committee activities and official functions
  • Volunteer activities at sanctioned QMA events

Cover does not apply to unsanctioned training, unofficial time trials, or activities at unapproved venues. Always check with the secretary if you're unsure whether a specific activity is covered.

Policy documents

The current Certificate of Currency and policy schedule are available from the QMA secretary on request. The policy is renewed annually by AMA and the details are updated on this page each year.

Making a claim

If an incident occurs at a QMA event that may give rise to a public liability claim, report it to the Meet Director immediately and document the circumstances in writing. Do not admit liability on behalf of QMA. Contact the secretary as soon as possible after the event. Claims are managed through AMA's insurer.
