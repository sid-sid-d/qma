Toowoomba

High altitude, cool conditions, and a masters athletics group that's been quietly competitive for decades.

Toowoomba sits more than 600 metres above sea level on the Darling Downs escarpment, and that altitude is not an incidental detail. It means cooler conditions year-round, less humidity than the coast, and a training environment that genuinely suits distance running. Athletes who train at altitude and then compete at sea level carry a measurable physiological advantage, and the Toowoomba group knows it. Several members compete regularly at state and national championships, and a few have represented Australia at World Masters Athletics.

The group is small and has been that way for years. That keeps the coaching attention high and the session atmosphere close-knit. Track and field at Clive Berghofer Stadium covers the full range of disciplines, with the Saturday morning session being the primary meeting point for all event groups. If you're based in Toowoomba and serious about your athletics, this is the group to be part of.

Clive Berghofer Stadium

Toowoomba is actively looking for a session coordinator to help expand to a mid-week session. If you're based in Toowoomba and have the time and interest to help build the group, get in touch with QMA. You don't need coaching qualifications to coordinate a session. QMA provides support and resources to help local groups get established.
