Track and field is a coached sport. The athletes on your local start line didn't get there alone, and neither will you. Every structured training option available through the QMA network is listed below.

Moran & Burgess Coaching Lisa Moran & Kate Burgess · Sprint & Speed, Brisbane
Featured Coaches
Sprints Speed Development Masters Specialist AA Level 2 Certified

"Most of our athletes in the 30-40 bracket arrive with the same belief: that they've missed their window. That competitive athletics was something they should have kept up in their twenties, and now it's too late to be any good. Between the two of us, we've made it our business to prove that wrong."

Lisa came back to competitive athletics at 34, after nearly a decade away. Full-time project management role, two daughters under five, and the kind of accumulated stiffness that makes you question whether you stretched properly in your twenties. Kate never really stopped competing, but shifted from open to masters athletics at 37 when she realised the age-group rankings suited her considerably better than the open field.

Together we coach athletes who are serious about performance without sacrificing the rest of their lives to get it. The 30-40 age bracket is our favourite group to work with. They have life discipline, they follow programs, they actually listen to feedback, and they bring a focus to sessions that twenty-two-year-olds rarely manage.

What athletes in this bracket need is different, not less. More deliberate warm-up protocols. Targeted strength work alongside track sessions. A training schedule that respects the reality that you have a job, possibly kids, and a life outside the track. Our approach is periodised and evidence-based: a 16-week competition preparation cycle with a base phase, a development phase, and a competition sharpening phase.

For athletes new to masters athletics, we typically recommend starting with two quality sessions per week and being intentional about a third easy recovery session. Within six months, most athletes in the 30-40 bracket are running faster times than they expected, often faster than they ran at university. That tends to be the moment they're fully converted.

If you're sitting on the fence about whether this is for you: come to a session. We'll show you what your legs are actually capable of right now.
