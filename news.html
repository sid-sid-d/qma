Competition

State Championships: Brisbane 2026

QMA's biggest meet of the year drew 340 athletes across all age groups and disciplines. Six new Queensland records were set across sprints, hurdles, and field events. The depth of competition in the 35 to 45 age brackets was unlike anything seen at a state championships in recent years.

Records

New QLD Record: W45 3000m Steeplechase

Diane Walsh breaks the Queensland record in the women's 45-49 steeplechase with a time of 12:14.6 at the QSAC Nathan April meet. The previous record had stood for nine years. Walsh, who came to steeplechase from road running three seasons ago, says the water jump used to terrify her.

Nationals

Nationals: Queensland Takes Three Medals

Three QMA athletes return from the Australian Masters Athletics Championships with medals in throws and distance events. The results place Queensland among the strongest state programs in the country for the M50 and W40 categories. Full results and athlete profiles inside.

Volunteer spotlight

Volunteer Spotlight: Robyn Cassidy

QMA's longest-serving official talks about 22 years of starting guns, bad weather, and why she keeps coming back. Robyn has started more than 4,000 races across her QMA career and says she has no plans to stop. "The athletes keep me young," she says. "Especially the 30-year-olds who think they're the first ones to discover this sport."

Member highlight

From Couch to State Championships in 18 Months

How Jess Chen went from recreational runner to W35 100m title holder. Eighteen months ago, Jess had never worn spikes. Now she's on the podium at the state championships and training twice a week with the QMA Brisbane sprint group. Her advice to anyone sitting on the fence: "Just show up once. Once is all it takes."

2026 Season Preview: What's New This Year

New events on the calendar, updated age grading tables, and what to expect from the QMA committee in 2026. This season introduces a dedicated W30 and M30 category across more events, a new cross country venue on the Sunshine Coast, and the return of the combined pentathlon series. Here's everything you need to know before the first gun fires.
