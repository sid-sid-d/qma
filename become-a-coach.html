Become a Coach

Athletics coaching is one of the most rewarding things you can do with your competition knowledge. And QMA needs more coaches.

Why coach?

Masters athletes carry something that's genuinely rare: technical knowledge built over years of competition, combined with the perspective of someone who has trained through setbacks, come back from injury, and kept showing up anyway. That knowledge belongs in front of athletes who are just starting out. Coaching is how it gets there.

Being a coach keeps you sharp. You have to articulate things you've done by instinct for years. You have to watch movement and recognise what's wrong before you can say what's right. That process makes you a better athlete too. And staying connected to the track, to the community, and to the sport in a coaching role extends the life of your involvement in ways that competing alone doesn't.

The accreditation pathway

Athletics Australia runs a tiered coaching accreditation system. Level 1 is a two-day course covering practical coaching skills and the fundamentals of athletics theory. It's designed to be accessible to people with competition experience who have never formally coached before. Level 2 is more advanced, with event-specific specialisation modules available across sprints, distance, jumps, throws, and hurdles.

QMA can connect prospective coaches with existing accredited coaches for mentoring before, during, and after formal courses. We can also assist with applications and point you toward the next available course dates in Queensland. The Athletics Australia website lists course schedules for all states.

What QMA needs right now

QMA has good coaching coverage in sprints and distance, but specific gaps exist that we're actively trying to fill:

  • Field event coaches: jumps and throws coaching is thin outside Brisbane, and we have interested athletes in these events across multiple regions who lack local coaching access.
  • Distance coaches outside Brisbane: the Gold Coast and Sunshine Coast have active members who are training without structured coaching support.
  • Women coaches: QMA is actively seeking women coaches at all levels. If you're a female athlete with competition experience and any interest in coaching, please reach out.
  • Coaches for intro sessions: new members and first-time athletes benefit enormously from an initial guided session before they join the general squad. Any Level 1 coach can run these.

Getting started

Talk to QMA first. Reach out via email or speak to one of the coaches at your next session. We'll discuss what events you'd coach, what accreditation support is available, and how to fit coaching into your existing involvement with the club. There's no commitment required at that stage. Just a conversation.

Contact us at secretary@qldmastersathletics.org.au and mention coaching in your subject line.
