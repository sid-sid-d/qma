For Every Adult
Who is adult track & field for?

Masters athletics isn't about being the fastest (although we're more than happy to race you). It's about improvement, staying active, and competing on your own terms. We compete in five-year age groups, so you're always matched against peers at your stage of life.

Whether you're chasing a personal best, nailing a technique you've spent a decade on, or just getting off the couch, you'll find your place here.

Performance Grading
Goals for every decade

Masters athletics uses age grading: a scoring system that converts your performance into a percentage of the world record for your exact age. It makes goal setting meaningful.

A 52-year-old running 100m in 13.2 seconds is performing at over 85% of the age-adjusted world record. It's an outstanding achievement for a body that carries more accumulated wear and tear compared to a young professional athlete that can dedicate themselves to their sport. Age grading puts every athlete on a level playing field, and gives you a number to chase that has nothing to do with how fast you were at 22.

Example: A 45-year-old woman runs 14.0 seconds for 100m. Her age-graded score is ~83%: national class. The open world record is 10.49s, but adjusted for her age, she's performing at a world-class level.

Where We Compete
Compete where the best do.

Queensland Sport and Athletics Centre (QSAC) is the state's premier athletics facility: a world-class stadium in Nathan, Brisbane. It's where Australian Olympians train, where records are broken, and where QMA members compete every season.

This is nothing like your hometown's school oval. When you pull on a race bib at a QMA meet, you're competing on the same track that has hosted Australian Athletics Championships, international meets, and some of the fastest performances ever recorded on Australian soil. And with new surfaces laid in 2026, it's now springier than ever.

Location: Kessells Road, Nathan, Brisbane
Track: World Athletics Class 1 certified 400m track
Facilities: Full field event infrastructure, grandstand seating
Also competing: Gold Coast, Sunshine Coast, Toowoomba, FNQ
