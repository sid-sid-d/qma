About

History

Fifty years of masters athletics in Queensland.

QMA was founded in 1975 by a small group of Brisbane athletes who believed competitive track and field shouldn't stop when open careers ended. What started with 12 founding members and a borrowed track has grown into Queensland's peak body for adult athletics, with hundreds of members competing across five decades.

The story of QMA is the story of masters athletics in this country: a sport built by people who refused to stop competing, run by volunteers who gave it structure and ambition, and shaped by athletes whose best performances were still ahead of them.

1975
Foundation

Queensland Masters Athletics Association founded in Brisbane. A founding committee of 12 athletes from the Brisbane athletics community came together with a shared conviction: competitive track and field was for adults, not just the young. The first meet was held at Perry Park, with 34 competitors across six age groups.

1978
First State Championships

QMA runs its first full state championships. Forty-seven competitors across eight age groups. W40 and M40 categories dominated early membership, reflecting a generation of athletes finding their way back to the track. The championships established a template for competition that still shapes the QMA calendar today.

1983
QSAC Nathan Opens

QMA moves primary operations to the new Queensland Sports and Athletics Centre at Nathan. The all-weather synthetic track transforms training and competition quality overnight. For the first time, Queensland masters athletes have access to a world-standard facility. The QSAC becomes the home of QMA and remains so today.

1987
First National Team

QMA sends its first official team to the Australian Masters Athletics Championships in Melbourne. Three Queensland athletes finish on the national podium. The result signals that Queensland masters athletics has matured from a local social sport into a genuine competitive force. National representation becomes a fixture of QMA's calendar.

1994
Women's Division Expands

Women's membership triples over the decade. New age groups and events are added to accommodate growing female participation, including women's hammer and pole vault. QMA becomes one of the first Queensland athletics bodies to offer a full women's program across all event disciplines, a decision that reshapes the membership base permanently.

1999
World Championship Debut

Queensland athletes represent Australia at the World Masters Athletics Championships in Gateshead, UK. The first Queensland masters world medal arrives in the W50 1500m. Six QMA members make the trip, establishing a connection with the international masters community that has grown stronger with each subsequent world championships.

2005
Regional Expansion

Gold Coast and Sunshine Coast programs formally affiliate with QMA. For the first time, Queensland masters athletics has organised competition outside Brisbane. QMA membership passes 200 registered athletes for the first time. The expansion reflects a sport growing in confidence, reach, and the diversity of athletes it serves.

2010
Digital Results Launch

QMA moves to online results publication for the first time. Real-time results via the new website replace the printed sheets and manual posting that had served the sport since 1975. The digital shift accelerates in subsequent years, eventually enabling online entries, membership management, and the kind of transparency members now expect as standard.

2018
Record Membership

QMA reaches 420 registered members. The headline statistic is this: the youngest new members average age 34, reflecting a surge of interest from 30 to 40 year olds discovering the sport. Masters athletics is no longer predominantly a sport for athletes in their fifties and sixties. A new generation is arriving earlier.

2024
Next Chapter

New committee, new branding direction, new focus on 30 to 40 athlete recruitment. The sport's youngest age groups are now its fastest-growing. Masters athletics is no longer a retirement sport: it is adult athletics, and Queensland is helping to lead that shift. Fifty years in, the ambition is the same as it was at Perry Park in 1975. Compete. Improve. Keep going.
